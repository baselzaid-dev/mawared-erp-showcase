# Mawared ERP — Public Product Roadmap

This roadmap communicates product direction without publishing private delivery dates, customer information, credentials, or implementation details. Priorities may change after testing and pilot feedback.

## Phase 1 — Core platform foundation

- Android application architecture and Arabic RTL interface.
- Backend API and PostgreSQL data layer.
- Authentication and session handling.
- Multi-company foundations and role-based access control.
- Employee and department management.
- Staging deployment and health verification.

**Status:** Active and undergoing continued verification.

## Phase 2 — HR workflows

- Attendance and absence workflows.
- Break and work-status requests.
- Leave creation, review, and multi-stage approval.
- Manager, HR, and administrator permission boundaries.
- HR operational reports and audit-oriented history.

**Status:** Incremental implementation and end-to-end testing.

## Phase 3 — Business operations

- Payroll processing and payroll records.
- Purchasing requests and approval workflows.
- Supplier management.
- Finance-facing operational integration.
- Internal tasks, training, and communication modules.

**Status:** Planned and partially represented in the product experience; backend delivery is incremental.

## Phase 4 — Production readiness

- Security review and remediation.
- Rate limiting, validation, logging, and audit improvements.
- Backup and disaster-recovery procedures.
- Performance and reliability testing.
- Monitoring, alerting, and operational runbooks.
- Controlled company pilot and feedback cycle.

**Status:** Preparation in parallel with feature development.

## Phase 5 — Commercial expansion

- Configurable company onboarding.
- Subscription and edition management.
- Optional HR-only and full ERP editions.
- Broader reporting and management dashboards.
- Additional mobile and web experiences based on validated demand.

**Status:** Product direction; scope will be finalized after pilot validation.

## Release principle

Mawared ERP will not treat a visually complete screen as a production-ready feature. A module is considered ready only after its backend rules, permissions, data persistence, error handling, tests, and deployment behavior have been verified together.
